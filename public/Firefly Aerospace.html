Aerospace Engineering

Firefly Aerospace: Propulsion Engineer

3+ years designing a 230,000 lbf rocket engine from blank sheet to flight hardware.

After graduating from George Mason University, I moved to Austin, Texas to join Firefly Aerospace, a commercial launch startup building America's next generation of orbital rockets. I joined with two requirements: startup, and building something extraordinary. Firefly delivered on both.

I began in the Propulsion department working on the Reaver Alpha main engine, quickly taking ownership of critical components and managing multiple design configurations ahead of Firefly's first commercial customer launch. Within months, I transitioned to Firefly's new MLV program.

As Engine Design Lead for the Miranda program, I maintained the master CAD assembly in Siemens NX, established the WBS and part numbering scheme, and served as the primary design authority, reviewing and approving nearly every component integrated into the engine. I was solely responsible for the complete thrust chamber design: 100% of the CAD models and fabrication drawings from blank sheet through release.

In my final role as Propulsion Planner, I owned production tracking for all Miranda engine builds through Flight 1, built a custom React dashboard integrating PLM, ERP, and project management data, and maintained critical path analyses for program leadership. I ran 3× weekly cross-functional standups across design, manufacturing, and management.
