Belt Printer: Infinite-Z 3D Printer

A large-format 3D printer with an infinite Z-axis, designed to print full-scale solar car body panels in carbon fiber and PETG.

A persistent challenge in solar car construction is producing large, complex body panels affordably. Belt Printer was designed to solve that problem, a custom FDM 3D printer that prints onto a moving conveyor belt, enabling continuous extrusion along an effectively unlimited Z-axis while providing a 1m × 1m XY build area.

This was the second-generation iteration of our large-scale 3D printing effort. The design uses carbon fiber and PETG for structural and aerodynamic parts, combining low material cost with sufficient stiffness for solar car applications.

I led the design effort, producing a 100% parametric CAD model in OnShape while the team began machining linear rail components. The project was an exercise in designing custom motion systems, managing material-process tradeoffs, and leading fabrication planning with a student team.
