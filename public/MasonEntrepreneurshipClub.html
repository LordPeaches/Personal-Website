Mason Entrepreneurship Club: Founder

Launched GMU's first student entrepreneurship organization from scratch as a freshman, at the request of the university's Office of Entrepreneurship.

As a freshman at George Mason University, I was approached by the head of the Office of Entrepreneurship and Innovation to build a new student-run entrepreneurs club from the ground up. With no existing framework to build on, I created the organization's structure, recruited founding members, and established the club's programming.

Within one semester, we were holding weekly meetings with dozens of regular attendees and bringing in prominent local founders and investors to speak at club events. The club became a hub for students interested in startups and innovation at GMU.

I transitioned leadership to a successor once Hypernova Solar reached a scale that demanded my full attention. The club continued operating under new leadership following my departure.
