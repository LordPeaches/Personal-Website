Arctos Robotic Arm: 6-DOF Robotic Arm

A solo robotics build exploring 6-axis motion, embedded control, and precision CAD from the ground up.

In the Fall of 2025, I took on this project as a solo endeavor to expand my engineering skillset beyond aerospace into robotics. Based on the open-source Arctos Arm project, my goals were threefold: develop a foundational understanding of robotic kinematics and control theory, gain hands-on experience with robotics controller architecture, and put my CAD and 3D printing skills to work on a mechanically complex system.

The original open-source design was distributed in Fusion 360 format and was in relatively rough condition — loosely constrained geometry, inconsistent design intent, and a bill of materials that was largely obsolete and sourced through Alibaba vendors with poor US availability. I rebuilt the entire assembly from scratch in OnShape, establishing clean parametric relationships and modifying key components to accommodate readily available domestic hardware and fasteners.

From first print to a fully commissioned, moving arm took approximately two months. The project gave me practical experience in stepper motor control, joint-space kinematics, and embedded firmware — and reinforced that the most valuable part of any build is the design work that makes it actually repeatable and maintainable.
