Robotics Mentorship

FRC Team 1895 — Where It All Started

The team that sparked my passion for engineering — and where I still give back as a mentor.

FIRST Robotics Competition (FRC) is one of the most intense and rewarding engineering experiences a high schooler can have. Teams of students have 6 weeks to design, build, and program a robot weighing up to 125 lbs — then compete against hundreds of other teams in alliance-based, 3v3 matches.

Team 1895 is where I first discovered my passion for engineering. The process of building something real under a hard deadline — the engineering, the iteration, the competition floor pressure — was immediately addicting.

I've returned as a mentor every season since graduating high school, giving back to the team that gave me my start. Mentoring here has sharpened my ability to communicate complex engineering concepts clearly, lead students through design decisions, and build culture on a technical team.
